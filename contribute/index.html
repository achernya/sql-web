sql.mit.edu is a volunteer-run service

Like all other SIPB services, we provide sql.mit.edu because we think it's a useful service for the MIT community. Like other student groups, SIPB has an annual budget, but it's not enough for us to do all of the improvements we have planned. Your donation will help improve not just sql.mit.edu, but also the other SIPB services, as well as help us start new, interesting and useful services for the MIT community.

How can I help?

There are lots of ways! Here are just some of the ways you can help out. Contact us if you have an idea that's not listed.

  • I'm a student – the best way you can help is by joining, either the sql.mit.edu project, or SIPB in general. We'd love to have new contributors to all our projects and services. Send us an email to get involved!
  • I'd like to make a cash donation – You can make a cash donation to SIPB through the MIT Giving Office. Your donation will help SIPB run hackathons, buy servers, equipment, replacement parts, and much more. Make sure to specify your donation is for the Student Information Processing Board Fund, account number 2721388.
  • I'd like to donate equipment – please contact us to discuss an equipment donation and to arrange for a SIPB member to accept delivery.
